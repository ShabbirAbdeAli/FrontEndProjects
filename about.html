About Azerbaijan

Located at the crossroads of Eastern Europe and Western Asia, Azerbaijan is a fascinating country with a rich history and vibrant culture. It's known as the "Land of Fire," a nod to its status as a birthplace of the Zoroastrian religion, which held fire as a symbol of divinity.

History

Azerbaijan's history stretches back thousands of years, as evidenced by the cave etchings of Gobustan Rock Art Cultural Landscape and the ancient Maiden Tower in the heart of Baku. From the medieval palaces of the Shirvanshahs to the modern, swooping architecture of the Heydar Aliyev Center, the country's built environment tells the story of a place at the crossroads of countless civilizations and cultures.

Culture & Traditions

The culture of Azerbaijan is as diverse as its landscape. Azerbaijanis have a rich tradition of folklore, music, dance, and cuisine. At the center of cultural celebrations are the Novruz festivities, which celebrate the Persian New Year and the arrival of spring. Azerbaijani music is notable for its soulful melodies and strong vocal performances, embodied in the traditional art of mugham.

Landscapes

The landscapes of Azerbaijan are incredibly diverse. From the sandy beaches of the Absheron Peninsula to the high peaks of the Greater Caucasus mountain range, and from the flaming hillsides of Yanar Dag to the thick forests of Goygol National Park, there's something for every nature lover in Azerbaijan.
